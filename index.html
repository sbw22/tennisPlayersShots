The Best Tennis Players for Each Shot
In tennis, there are some players that are known for having mastered certain shots. In this article, we will explain which player is the best at what.
Forehand
These players are known for their increadible forehands, which they used to dominate points with.

  • Juan Martín del Potro

    Juan Martín del Potro has one of the best forehands in the history of tennis. Few can match the power he generates on this stroke. He uses an eastern grip that he generates a flat ball with, and uses minimal wrist movement on his take-back and general motion of the swing to add to the flatness of his ball. Along with his 6'6 height, it is easy to see why Quan Martín del Potro has one of the best forehands in the game!

  • Rafael Nadal

    Rafael Nadal has arguably one of the most unusual forehands in the game, but you cannot deny its results! Nadal generates the most topspin on the tour with his forehand, often bringing the racket above his head to generate his heavy balls. Nadal also uses a semi-western grip.

  • Jack Sock

    Jack Sock has one of the most unconventional, but powerful, forehands in tennis. Even though his shots appear to be very flat, he actually generates some of the most spin on the tour! he has a condensed take-back and uses a full western grip. People have often critisized his forehand for having bad form, but you certainly can't argue with his results!

  • Fernando Gonzalez

    Fernando Gonzalez is known for having destructive forehands that dominate points, and for good reason! He is able to generate huge pace with his big take-back, semi-western grip, and technically sound stroke.

Backhand
These players are known for their dominating backhands, which they used to beat down their opponents with and dominate ponts.

  • Jannik Sinner

    Jannik Sinner is one of the youngest players on the ATP Tour, but that doesn't stop him from having one of the biggest backhands on the tour! He is in the top five in both rpm and speed on his backhand! With a strong coil and loose wrist, it is easy to see how he hits such an explosive backhand!

  • Novak Djokovic

    Novak Djokovic has arguably one of the greatest two-handed backhands in the history of tennis. His perfectly technically sound stroke includes a great hip and shoulder turn, balance, and use of the lower body.

  • John Millman

    John Millman dominates matches with his huge backhand that is in the top five in both rpm and speed. He uses a high take-back and a loose wrist to generate both speed and spin.

  • Roger Federer

    Roger Federer is argued to be the greatest player of all time, and a great reason for this is his backhand. An advantage of his one handed backhand is he can easily disguise his backhands and slices. He uses an eastern grip for his backhand and changes his grip slightly when he hits a slice. Key points for Federer's backhand are his preperation, footwork, position at contact, and contact point.

Serve
These players are known for having a crushing serve, acing many opponents and having a very high serve percentage.

  • Pete Sampras

    Pound for pound, Pete Sampras has one of the best serves of all time. Sampras uses a set up to the serve that many great players have copied, including Roger Federer. It allows him to set a strong base and explode to the ball. Sampras's serve is also easily reconizable by the prolonged pronation.

  • Ivo Karlovic

    Ivo Karlovic is one of the greatest servers of all time, if not the greatest. His fastest serve has come at a blistering 156 mph, and averages a whopping number of 19.8 aces a match. A few key characteristics of his serve are is ball toss, back-and-forth motion, and pronation.

  • John Isner

    John Isner has one of the best first serves in tennis history. His fastest recorded serve is 157.2 mph, and his first serve percentage is an astounding 72 percent! Along with his height, he has a fluid motion and low ball toss to help him get power and angles off his serve.

  • Goran Ivanišević

    Goran Ivanišević's serve was a weapon feared by everyone he played. He holds the record for the most aces in a season with 1,477 aces. He consistantly hits 133 mph serves and once hit 46 aces in the second round of Wimbledon in 1997. He incorporates a low ball toss and an early hit of the ball (sometimes before it hits the peak) in his deadly serve.

Volley/Net
These players are known for having an amazing net game, with excellent racket skills and dominating at the net.

  • Stefan Edberg

    Stefan Edberg was known to be the best serve and volleyer of all time. His serve was certainly a big factor in this, but his volleys were on a level by themself. his volleys had world class technique, reflexes, instincts, and more at the net. Using his serve and volley game, he won 6 grand slams, 4 Davis Cup titles, and more.

  • Michael Llodra

    Michael Llorda has one of the best volleys of all time, and was one of the last pure serve and volleyers. A defining part of Llodra's game is his high backhand volley, which he used to grab 3 grand slam championships and a silver medal.

  • Roger Federer

    Roger Federer is argued to be the greatest player of all time, and his volleys certainly back up these claims. Although he does not come to the net as his primary way to finish points, they are certainly on another level. He has world class footwork, racket positioning, and balance. However, a signature part of Federer's volley is his head position. His head stays perfectly still as he hits the ball and his eyes stay glued onto the ball until contact point.

  • Leander Paes

    Leander Paes is one of the biggest names in doubles, and his volley is a huge reason for this. He holds the record for the most mens doubles grand slam titles at 18. He has excellent balance, footwork, and instincts that help him dominate on the court.
